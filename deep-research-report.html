Is Apple a Good Stock to Buy?

📅 2026-06-03📋 1 SEC filing (10-Q)🌐 22 web sources

Apple (AAPL) is a conditionally attractive investment — exceptional business fundamentals, a credible AI growth thesis, and dominant ecosystem lock-in are offset by a historically stretched valuation, material China and tariff exposure, and an AI execution test that culminates at WWDC on June 8, 2026. As of June 3, 2026, the stock trades at $310.26 with a $4.5 trillion market cap and a Wall Street consensus "Buy" rating, but the average analyst price target of $310.51 implies virtually zero near-term upside at current prices. Whether Apple is a good buy depends heavily on your investment horizon, risk tolerance, and conviction in the AI narrative.

Primary Source: SEC Form 10-Q (Q2 FY2026)

The authoritative primary source for Apple's financials is its Form 10-Q filed with the SEC on May 1, 2026 for the quarter ended March 28, 2026 (SEC EDGAR: CIK 0000320193, filing date May 1, 2026). The filing is also available as an as-filed PDF and verified through StockTitan's SEC filing mirror.

Income Statement (Condensed Consolidated Statements of Operations)

The 10-Q's Condensed Consolidated Statements of Operations record the following for the three months ended March 28, 2026 (Q2 FY2026) vs. the year-ago period (three months ended March 29, 2025):

  • Total net sales: $111,184 million vs. $95,359 million (+17% YoY). Products: $80,208M vs $68,714M; Services: $30,976M vs $26,645M.
  • Net income: $29,578 million vs. $24,780 million (+19% YoY).
  • Diluted EPS: $2.01 vs. $1.65 (+22% YoY).
  • Gross margin: $54,781 million (49.3%) vs. $44,867 million (47.1%). Products margin 38.7%; Services margin 76.7%.
  • Six-month cumulative net sales: $254,940 million vs. $219,659 million, with net income of $71,675M vs $61,110M and diluted EPS of $4.85 vs $4.05.

  • Total term debt: $8,310M current + $74,404M non-current = $82,714M outstanding.

Cash Flow Statement (Six Months Ended March 28, 2026)

  • Operating cash flow: $82,627 million in the first two quarters of FY2026 — an annualised rate exceeding $160 billion.
  • Share repurchases: $36,989 million of common stock repurchased in the first half of FY2026 alone, before the additional $100B authorization.

MD&A: Tariff Risk Factor (verbatim)

Apple's own Management Discussion & Analysis section carries this verbatim risk disclosure on tariffs:

"Tariffs and other measures that are applied to the Company's products or their components can have a material adverse impact on the Company's business, results of operations and financial condition, including impacting the Company's supply chain, the availability of rare earths and other raw materials and components, pricing and gross margin."

The filing further specifies the scope of new tariff actions:

"Beginning in the second quarter of 2025, new tariffs were announced on imports to the U.S., including additional tariffs on imports from China, India, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, Vietnam and the European Union ('EU'), among others. In response, several countries have imposed, or threatened to impose, reciprocal tariffs on imports from the U.S. and other retaliatory measures."

"Greater China net sales increased during the second quarter of 2026 compared to the same periods in 2025 due to higher net sales of iPhone. The strength in the renminbi relative to the U.S. dollar had a favorable year-over-year impact on Greater China net sales during the second quarter of 2026."

Recent Financial Performance

Apple's operating momentum is strong and accelerating. In its most recent quarter (Q2 FY2026, ended March 28, 2026), Apple reported revenue of $111.2 billion, up 17% year-over-year, its best March quarter ever. Diluted EPS reached $2.01, up 22% from $1.65 in Q2 2025, with an overall gross margin of 49.3%. The Services segment hit an all-time high of $31 billion, up 16% year-over-year, with a Services gross margin of 76.7%.

For the full fiscal year 2025, Apple posted revenue of $416.2 billion with net income of $112 billion, with Services crossing the $100 billion annual revenue milestone for the first time, up 14%. The company's balance sheet is robust, with a net cash position of $62 billion and a fresh $100 billion share buyback authorization and 4% dividend increase. For Q3 FY2026, management guided for 14–17% year-over-year revenue growth, significantly ahead of analyst expectations of 9–10%.

Analyst Consensus and Price Targets

Wall Street is broadly bullish on Apple. Based on 48 analysts polled by S&P Global — 23 Strong Buy, 7 Buy, 15 Hold, 2 Sell, 1 Strong Sell — the consensus rating is Buy with an average price target of $310.51, implying essentially flat upside from the current price.

The most bullish voice is Wedbush's Daniel Ives, who raised his target to $400 in May 2026, calling Apple "the sleeping tech giant about to see a major inflection point" and projecting that roughly 20% of the world's population will access AI through an Apple device over coming years. Bank of America's Wamsi Mohan (target: $380) argues that "Apple does not need to win the artificial intelligence model competition to come out ahead in the next phase of AI development." Morgan Stanley's Erik Woodring (target: $330, bull case: $440) identified WWDC 2026 as a "key catalyst for Apple stock, potentially reframing the company as an 'AI winner'" with the bull-case path to $440 contingent on a polished AI platform announcement.

The most notable bear on the street is Barclays analyst Tim Long, who maintains an Underweight rating with a $253 target, citing ongoing uncertainty in the AI strategy and margin risk. Long stated: "The firm said it remains cautious given uncertainty around rising costs and an unclear AI strategy."

Growth Drivers

Apple Intelligence and AI

Apple's AI platform — Apple Intelligence — is gaining adoption rapidly. More than 80% of Apple Silicon-eligible devices have now activated Apple Intelligence features. Apple's partnership with Google, struck in January 2026, powers a rebuilt Siri with Gemini. The joint statement read: "After careful evaluation, we determined that Google's technology provides the most capable foundation for Apple Foundation Models." The rebuilt Siri is expected to debut in iOS 27 in September 2026 following its WWDC unveiling on June 8. Wedbush projects $15 billion in additional annual services revenue from AI-driven subscriptions as AI monetization matures.

Services Flywheel

Services is Apple's most valuable growth engine. Apple now has over 1.1 billion paid subscriptions across its platform, with the App Store alone drawing more than 850 million average weekly users globally. The installed base underpinning this ecosystem stands at more than 2.5 billion active devices. Services gross margins of 76.7% dwarf the 38.7% margins on Apple's physical products, making every incremental Services dollar extremely valuable.

India Manufacturing and Market Expansion

India has become a critical pillar of Apple's supply chain and growth story. Apple assembled 55 million iPhones in India in 2025, a 53% jump, and India now represents 25% of global iPhone production. Tim Cook has expressed extraordinary enthusiasm: "I'm over the moon excited about India." India serves simultaneously as a manufacturing hedge against China tariff risk and a massive untapped consumer market where, Cook notes, the majority of buyers are new to Apple products.

Key Investment Risks

China and Tariff Exposure

China represents Apple's single largest geographic risk. In Q2 FY2026, Greater China contributed $20.5 billion in revenue — but the path has been volatile. Earlier in FY2025, China revenue fell 11.1% year-over-year in Q1 FY2025, the steepest decline since 2023, partly because Apple Intelligence was blocked in mainland China pending regulatory approval. With approximately 90% of iPhones still assembled in China, tariffs could cost Apple an estimated $8.5 billion per year (Morgan Stanley estimate), and Tim Cook warned in May 2025 that tariffs would cost Apple at least $900 million in a single quarter.

Regulatory and Antitrust Risk

Apple faces a three-front regulatory battle. In the US, the DOJ antitrust lawsuit is advancing — a federal judge ruled in June 2025 that Apple's alleged 65% US smartphone market share and 70% performance smartphone market share are sufficient to allege monopoly power, allowing the case to proceed toward a trial expected no earlier than 2028. In Europe, the EU's Digital Markets Act has already resulted in a €500 million fine for breaching the DMA's anti-steering obligation. Critically, the DOJ is also appealing the Google search default ruling, which threatens Apple's $15–20 billion annual Google search deal — a JPMorgan analysis estimates a potential $12.5 billion annual revenue hit in the worst case.

Valuation Premium

Apple's premium valuation is the most commonly cited bear argument. The stock currently trades at a trailing P/E of 38.2x and forward P/E of 34.6x, with a PEG ratio of 3.08. This represents a significant premium to Apple's own history — Apple's current P/E is 41% above its 10-year median of 26.56x. Compared to peers, the premium is stark: Microsoft trades at a forward P/E of 23.1x, Alphabet at 28.7x, and Meta at just 18.2x — all with comparable or faster earnings growth than Apple's. Raymond James analyst Srini Pajjuri, resuming coverage in January 2026 with a cautious Market Perform rating, noted: "the current stock price already incorporates the optimism surrounding its AI roadmap" — and the stock has since risen ~15% above that level.

Hardware Saturation and Lengthening Upgrade Cycles

iPhone upgrade cycles continue to stretch. 42% of US iPhone replacements in the 12 months ending September 2025 involved devices owned for 3+ years, up from 32% the prior year. The average traded-in iPhone in the US is now nearly 3.6 years old. While AI-driven refresh cycles could reverse this trend — Q1 FY2026 iPhone revenue surged 23% year-over-year — Raymond James projects iPhone unit growth of only 3% in both 2026 and 2027 on a volume basis.

Market Sentiment and Institutional Activity

AAPL closed at $310.26 on June 3, 2026 — up 14.12% year-to-date and 52.63% over the prior twelve months. The 52-week range runs from $195.07 to $315.20. Short interest is benign at 0.95% of float with 2.7 days to cover, indicating bears are not pressing the position. Institutional ownership stands at 67.73%, with $287 billion in institutional inflows vs. $131 billion in outflows over the past 12 months — net buying. Renaissance Technologies notably opened a new $770 million Apple position in Q1 2026.

The most significant institutional overhang is Berkshire Hathaway's continued reduction of its Apple stake. Under Warren Buffett, Berkshire sold 75% of its Apple position over nine quarters, from a peak of 915 million shares (worth ~$174 billion) to approximately 228 million shares (worth ~$59–62 billion) by the end of 2025. Buffett's rationale, consistent with his broader value philosophy, appears to be that he bought at 10–15x earnings and sold as it approached 34–38x.

Near-Term Catalyst: WWDC 2026 (June 8)

The most important near-term event for Apple investors is WWDC 2026, beginning June 8. Morgan Stanley described it as a "key catalyst" with low expectations creating a favorable setup for a narrative re-rating, with potential to push valuation to $365–$385 and, in a bull case, $440. The conference is expected to unveil a rebuilt agentic Siri (powered by Google Gemini), iOS 27, and Apple's vision for on-device and cloud AI. As one analysis described it: "It's not a stretch to call this year's WWDC the most significant software announcement in recent history." If Siri lands as a capable AI assistant, it could accelerate the upgrade cycle and re-rate the stock toward the high-end analyst targets.

Verdict

Apple is a high-quality business at a premium price. The financial fundamentals are exceptional — record revenues, expanding margins, Services growing at 76%+ gross margins, and a cash-generative balance sheet. The AI thesis is credible and WWDC on June 8 represents a significant near-term catalyst. However, several factors create real caution:

  • Valuation: At 38x trailing and 35x forward earnings with a PEG of 3.08, Apple is priced for perfection and trades at a ~41% premium to its own 10-year median P/E of ~26x.
  • Analyst target saturation: The average price target of $310.51 essentially equals the current price — consensus has largely priced in the bull case.
  • China and tariff risk: ~90% of iPhone assembly in China, up to $8.5B in potential annual tariff costs, and continued AI restrictions in mainland China are material headwinds.
  • Regulatory exposure: A $12.5B worst-case revenue hit from the Google search deal, ongoing DOJ antitrust litigation, and EU DMA compliance costs are not trivial.
  • Berkshire selling: Warren Buffett trimmed 75% of his Apple position as the stock re-rated from 10–15x to 34–38x earnings — a notable value signal from history's most successful stock picker.

Bottom line: For long-term investors with a 3–5 year horizon and conviction in Apple's AI monetization story, the stock remains defensible — the Services business alone is a durable compounder. For near-term or value-oriented investors, the combination of a maxed-out consensus target, stretched valuation, and pending AI execution test makes current levels uninviting without a meaningful pullback or WWDC-driven catalyst.
